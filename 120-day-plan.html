120-Day Entry Plan

Entry principle

Earn the right to scale by making portfolio decisions, risk ownership, control evidence, and value realization legible before adding automation velocity.

Days 1-30 - Read the system

Portfolio and demand

  • Map current automation portfolio, pipeline, demand sources, and dispositions.
  • Understand how BPM leaders and business leaders prioritize work.
  • Identify recurring stalled, reworked, or under-adopted patterns.

Platforms and process intelligence

  • Learn current RPA/workflow platforms and selection history.
  • Assess process mining, task mining, monitoring, and telemetry availability.
  • Understand run ownership, maintenance, and incident patterns.

Risk and controls

  • Learn risk taxonomy, compliance obligations, control design, testing, escalation, and audit evidence expectations.
  • Clarify human authority and exception-handling requirements.

People and decision rights

  • Meet Process Engineers and adjacent disciplines.
  • Clarify COE, LOB, risk, technology, finance, and owner decisions.
  • Observe coaching, capacity, and skill opportunities without pre-judgment.

Outputs

  • Portfolio and stakeholder map; platform and process-intelligence inventory.
  • Initial decision-rights map and risk/control evidence map.
  • Baseline list of hypotheses and early opportunities.

Days 31-90 - Align and Prove

Days 31-60 - Align the decisions

Common intake

  • Name the member/employee outcome, owner, baseline, and process boundary.
  • Separate redesign, assist, automate, and standardize candidates.
  • Require assumptions and evidence rather than maturity theater.

Assurance Twin review

  • Pair workflow change with ownership, authority, evidence, monitoring, and fallback.
  • Define minimum review requirements by risk and operating mode.

Business-case discipline

  • Connect benefit to specific KPIs and a credible baseline.
  • Include maintenance, platform, exception, adoption, and control costs.
  • Assign value-realization ownership beyond go-live.

Team operating cadence

  • Install practical portfolio review and coaching cadence.
  • Use case reviews to build capability, not only approve work.
  • Create visible escalation and decision records.

Days 61-90 - Prove the mechanism

  • Apply the review to a bounded mix of current, proposed, and underperforming automations selected with USAA leaders.
  • Document what the model changes: decision, risk boundary, evidence, owner, business case, or operating mode.
  • Strengthen measurement and monitoring; close the gaps that materially affect sustainable scale.
  • Coach Process Engineers and partners through the real work; capture reusable learning without turning every case into bureaucracy.

Outputs

  • Validated intake and review mechanism.
  • Evidence-backed dispositions for selected cases.
  • Initial team capability plan and platform/monitoring improvement priorities.

Days 91-120 - Scale the Learning

Institutionalize what worked

Standards

  • Codify proven review patterns, evidence requirements, and exception rules.
  • Preserve legitimate LOB variation with explicit boundaries.

Platform strategy

  • Translate case evidence into tool-selection and platform-roadmap decisions.
  • Clarify where RPA, workflow, API integration, AI assistance, or process redesign fits.

Value governance

  • Create a quarterly portfolio view connecting realized value, control quality, adoption, and maintenance burden.
  • Make improve, standardize, and retire normal portfolio decisions.

Capability

  • Define coaching, mentoring, and technical-development priorities for Process Engineers.
  • Develop internal reviewers and champions to avoid a single-point-of-expertise COE.

120-day success evidence

  • Leaders can see why each priority automation is being redesigned, assisted, automated, standardized, improved, or retired.
  • Process Engineers use a shared decision language without reducing the work to compliance paperwork.
  • Risk and control evidence is visible early enough to shape design.
  • Business cases have named baselines, owners, measurement plans, and maintenance economics.
  • At least one proven pattern is ready for broader reuse, and at least one weaker candidate is productively reframed.

Operating discipline

Specific case counts, timelines, and target measures should be set with USAA after learning portfolio complexity and regulatory obligations. The plan defines a disciplined sequence that can adapt to the evidence.
