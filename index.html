Why this role matters now

Member value is becoming an operating discipline.

USAA's 2025 Annual Report describes a deliberate focus on simplifying digital experiences, improving claims processing, reducing paperwork, and making the enterprise work more seamlessly for members. It also created a Member Value organization to anticipate needs and remove friction.

14 million+members served across insurance, banking, and retirement solutions - a scale at which local automation choices become enterprise operating choices.
Faster service vs. stronger control

Automation must reduce repetitive effort without weakening evidence, exception handling, or human accountability.

Enterprise standards vs. business reality

A COE needs reusable governance while respecting legitimate variation across insurance, banking, claims, service, and shared functions.

Platform scale vs. process truth

RPA and workflow tools amplify whatever process logic they are given. Process intelligence must precede tool enthusiasm.

Savings vs. realized member value

A business case is incomplete until the benefit appears in cycle time, quality, service, risk, adoption, or another named outcome.

Candidate thesis

The Automation Assurance Twin

Every automation candidate should have two synchronized views: the work plane that changes the member or employee experience, and the assurance plane that defines ownership, authority, evidence, monitoring, and fallback. Scale only when both are ready.

Automation value scorecard

A business case that survives contact with operations.

The scorecard does not manufacture a maturity number. It names the evidence categories that allow BPM leaders, risk partners, process owners, and platform teams to make the same decision from the same facts.

Member valueWhich friction, delay, repetition, or uncertainty changes?Evidence: service outcome, wait/cycle time, complaint or repeat-contact signal.
Employee flowWhat work becomes easier, safer, or more focused?Evidence: touches, rework, queue age, exception burden, adoption.
Control qualityDoes the automation make authority and evidence more legible?Evidence: control coverage, traceability, exception closure, monitoring.
EconomicsIs value tied to a credible baseline and total operating cost?Evidence: labor hours avoided, cycle time, quality cost, platform/maintenance burden.
Learning and reuseWhat becomes a standard, and what remains workflow-specific?Evidence: reuse effort, change latency, repeated failure modes, retirement decision.

Questions for executive discovery

The questions that reveal the real mandate.

01 / PORTFOLIO

Where does USAA most need this role to change the automation portfolio: selection, delivery, controls, value realization, or adoption?

02 / MEMBER VALUE

Which member-friction outcomes are already shared across BPM, the Member Value organization, and business lines?

03 / DECISION RIGHTS

What decisions sit with the COE, process owners, risk partners, platform teams, and line-of-business leaders?

04 / PROCESS INTELLIGENCE

How mature are process mining, task mining, and continuous process monitoring, and where is evidence still assembled manually?

05 / CONTROLS

Which control-evidence or exception-management gaps most often delay an automation from pilot to sustainable scale?

06 / PEOPLE

What must the Process Engineers become better at over the next year: process discovery, business cases, platform fluency, risk partnership, coaching, or value realization?

07 / ECONOMICS

How does USAA validate that projected automation value becomes realized member, employee, risk, or financial value?

08 / SUCCESS

Twelve months from now, what visible change would make you say the automation COE is operating differently because this director joined?
