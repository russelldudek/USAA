USAA's Process Automation director has a consequential mandate: improve member and employee experiences through automation while making ownership, risk, and control evidence more visible. The role's emphasis on BPM strategy, Process Engineers, portfolio selection, RPA platforms, process intelligence, measurable business cases, and embedded regulatory expectations makes enterprise operating leadership central to the job.

I have spent my career making complex work operational. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, and high-reliability programs while building quality systems, root-cause practices, measures, and process controls. At Amazon, I built leadership routines, labor planning, throughput visibility, escalation, and standard work in a 24/7 operation supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. Today at Vape-Jet, I lead AI-first operating transformation across production, quality, support, engineering issue flow, ERP, CRM, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows.

My candidate vision proposes an Automation Assurance Twin: a paired view of the work plane that changes the member or employee experience and the assurance plane that defines ownership, human authority, evidence, monitoring, and fallback. It helps a COE decide whether to redesign, assist, automate, or standardize - and connects each decision to specific value and control evidence. The model and 120-day entry plan are available at russelldudek.github.io/USAA/.

What makes my fit distinctive is not a single platform name. It is a career spent leading the full system that allows platforms to create sustainable value: process truth, technical translation, controls, operating cadence, people development, adoption, and accountable outcomes. I would bring that enterprise perspective immediately, learn USAA's current automation stack and regulatory context quickly, and partner closely with platform and risk specialists while coaching Process Engineers through real portfolio decisions.

That operating range would shape my first 120 days: read the existing portfolio and decision rights, align selection and assurance criteria, prove the approach on a bounded mix of cases, and turn the learning into reusable standards and coaching capability.

I am based in Pittsburgh and have been planning a move to the Tampa/St. Petersburg region. The four-day in-office requirement therefore aligns with a move already under consideration, without dependence on relocation assistance.

I would value a conversation about where USAA most needs this leader to change the automation system: selection, controls, platform strategy, value realization, team capability, or the connections among all five.
